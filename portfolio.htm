Thesis: Gender Imbalance Among Interior Design Students

This study explores the reasons why male students are not enrolling in the interior design program at ASU and to what degree gender influences the selection of a major.

Research: Semiotics, Design, and Chairs

Semiotics, also known as the study of signs, has been around for centuries. This study looked at the birth of semiotics, the importance of signs in design, and the hidden signs of status in chairs.

Group Project: Laser Vest for SWAN Rehabilitation Center

I led a small group of industrial design graduate students in order to assist the needs of SWAN Rehab therapists and patients. We researched human factors and built a vest with a removable laser pointer to help stroke patients find balance and gain strength.

Research: The Fetishism of Vans Sneakers

Vans have represented the punk rock and skateboard sub-culture since the 1970s. More recently, the sneakers have become popular in the hip-hop community. This study investigates the obsession and brand fetishism of Vans sneakers.

Team Research: Charlie’s Café Research Analysis

Working as a team, my partner and I used observation as a research method to understand the different types of people that go to Charlie’s Café at ASU. We created typologies and key insights based on our observations.

Visual Ethnography: Interior Design Students at ASU

In order to gain information on my thesis topic, I produced a visual ethnography on the gender imbalance in the interior design program at ASU. I interviewed junior and senior students at their studio desks. This is just a portion of my findings.

Group Project: Follow the Fabric from A-Z

As part of a group project, my group and I formed a proposal for Patagonia to promote sustainability in Arizona. Our idea was to recycle cotton into new shirts, use a QR code to track the garment’s life cycle, and create awareness about destruction of landfills and pesticides.

Video: Chairs and What They Represent

As part of a reading and conference course, I researched high-back chairs and their culture meanings. Using Windows Movie Maker, I produced a video on the significance of chairs in three different time periods and cultures.

Team Research: Storyboard for Sparky’s Den

As a team, my partner and I interviewed students at the billiard area in Sparky’s Den at ASU. We transcribed our interviews, looked for patterns, and created typologies of the types of people who come to Sparky’s Den. We also made a storyboard of our insights, provided design solutions, and presented it in video form.

Artwork & Craftsmanship Examples

As an undergraduate student, I created multiple pieces of artwork for my classes as well as for pleasure. Examples of my artwork include architecture drawings, sketches, paintings, and building a functional lamp.

Computer-Aided Design Examples

I produced several designs as an undergraduate student using Photoshop, Illustrator, InDesign, formZ, and animation tools. Examples of my work include a CD cover, a chair design, a master bathroom design, etc.
